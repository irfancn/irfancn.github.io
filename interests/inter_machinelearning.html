Machine Learning

I started learning machine learning to apply some of the tools for the PhD topic. At that time, people were starting to integrate machine learning into fracture mechanics, including my guide. Since I like programming and image processing, this turned out to be a nice topic to connect them. I took 6 online courses in coursera by none other than the great Andrew G, and gained some fundamentals. The courses were well-organized with slides, talks and tasks. Later, I tried different packages for tracking and recognition out of interests. This made me realize that machine learning right now is basically like plumbing - take the package one needs and plug it, then one might have to tune it. Thats it. But, knowing the fundamentals helps and I hope it will in the future.
